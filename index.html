Don't Call It an Intro

Hi, I'm Richard. I've had a long and meandering path to this point. My early CS experience was in music, using sequencing software that was entirely MIDI parameter-based on a Goldstar 386/33 MHz. I grew up playing music, and the change from reading notation to programming synthesizers was jarring. I sort of packed that away until I was in college, when I took a web design and started learning C++. It was a while ago. Email was new.

Since I graduated I've worked in the musical instrument industry, doing endorsement marketing with artists and engineers who utilized certain musical instrument brands. I did that for a spell, and got hit with a re-org. In the years between then and now I've picked up other media production tools in addition to music, e.g., video capture/edit/production, presention design, and some e-Learning. I had done some coding by necessity and curiousity, applying it professionally as needed.

A Brief Explanation of IEMs

I mentioned above that I sell Sensaphonics in-ear monitors (aka IEMs) to musicians, audio engineers, and producers. What are IEMs?
Earphones.

More specifically, they're earphones that are designed to output sound inside the ear canal rather than on top of the ear or in the ear's concha. They also, ideally, isolate the ear canal from outside sound. Some are designed to do this using methods anyone can use (generic IEMs) and some are designed to fit a single customer (custom IEMs).

Think of custom earphones like a bespoke suit. Like a bespoke suit, it is designed to fit one person. Unlike a bespoke suit, no one else of nearly the same size can wear it.
